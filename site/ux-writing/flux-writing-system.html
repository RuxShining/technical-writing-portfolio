# FLUX UX Writing System ## Product Overview FLUX is a training and recovery platform designed for hybrid athletes who combine strength and endurance training. The platform helps athletes monitor: - Training load - Nutrition - Recovery metrics - HRV trends - Performance readiness The UX writing system supports technically literate users while avoiding overly motivational, vague, or wellness-focused language. --- ## Voice Principles ### Direct Users are performance-focused and expect clear feedback. Use: > Recovery score dropped 12% from baseline. Avoid: > Your body might need a little extra rest today. ### Technically Literate Use terminology familiar to athletes. Examples: - Zone 2 - HRV - Training load - Deload week Avoid unnecessary scientific jargon without context. ### Low Ego The platform provides guidance, not coaching decisions. Use: > Based on your recent load, recovery may be limited today. Avoid: > FLUX recommends complete rest today. --- # Onboarding Flow ## Welcome Screen ### Headline Built for athletes who train both strength and endurance. ### Body FLUX tracks recovery, training load, and nutrition across hybrid training styles because most fitness apps are built for only one. ### Primary Action Get Started --- ## Training Profile ### Headline What does your training currently include? ### Options - Strength Training - Running - Cycling - Swimming - CrossFit / Functional Fitness - Hyrox / OCR - Triathlon - Other ### Supporting Text Your selections help calibrate energy expenditure and recovery targets. --- ## Weekly Training Load ### Headline How many hours do you train each week? ### Supporting Text Include lifting, cardio, conditioning, and recovery sessions. ### Helper Text Targets automatically adjust as your training load changes. --- ## Goal Selection ### Performance Improve output, speed, or strength. ### Body Composition Adjust weight while maintaining performance. ### Recovery Reduce fatigue and improve adaptation. ### Maintenance Maintain your current fitness level. --- # Empty States ## No Training Sessions ### Message No training sessions yet. ### Description Add your first session to begin tracking training load and recovery trends. ### Action Log Session --- ## No Nutrition Data ### Message No nutrition data today. ### Description Log meals to compare intake against recovery and workload. ### Action Track Meal --- ## No Recovery Data ### Message Recovery data unavailable. ### Description Connect a compatible wearable to enable readiness scoring. ### Action Connect Device --- # Error Messages ## Wearable Sync Failure Couldn't sync wearable data. Try again. ## Session Overlap This session overlaps with an existing entry. ## Offline State No connection. Changes will sync automatically. ## Invalid Weight Enter a value between 20 and 500. ## Unsaved Session Session wasn't saved. Your data is still available locally. --- # Push Notifications ## Recovery Alert ### Title Recovery score below baseline ### Body HRV is down 14% from your 7-day average. Consider reducing intensity today. --- ## Weekly Summary ### Title Week 18 summary ### Body 5 sessions • 7h 42m • 18,200 kg total volume --- ## Nutrition Reminder ### Title Protein target incomplete ### Body You've logged 82g today. Current target: 145g. --- # Confirmation Dialogs ## Delete Session ### Title Delete this session? ### Body This removes the workout and associated load calculations from your history. ### Action Delete Session --- ## Reset Targets ### Title Reset nutrition targets? ### Body Custom values will be replaced with calculated targets based on your current profile. ### Action Reset Targets --- # Accessibility Standards The UX writing system follows these accessibility principles: - Error messages never rely on color alone - Navigation labels always contain text - Tooltips support screen readers - Avoid directional instructions - Use concise labels for wearable and recovery data --- # Design Intent The system intentionally avoids: - Empty motivational language - Artificial encouragement - Excessively casual tone - Overexplaining technical concepts The goal is to create a product experience that feels credible to experienced athletes while remaining accessible to newer users.
